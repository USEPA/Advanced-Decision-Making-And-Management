Preliminary - Input Data

ADAM is a tool that helps you build and solve supply chain management and design models.

The participants of a supply chain include consumers, suppliers, technology providers, and transportation providers.

To construct a design model, one needs the following input data:

Supplier DataCosts and capacities for suppliers
Consumer DataCosts and capacities for consumers
Tech DataCosts (operating and investment) and capacities for technology providers
Transport DataCosts and capacities for transportation providers

Given input data, a design model is solved to determine locations for new technologies and transactions of products that maximize the collective profit of all participants and that satisfy a given design budget.

Preliminary - Output Data

A design model provides the following output data:

Flow DataFlow supplied, consumed, and processed at different locations
Transport DataTransport of products between locations
Value DataValue of products at different locations
Design DataOptimal location for new technologies

Once you have created a model, you can save it so that this can be duplicated and loaded to change features.

Here we specify the type of supply chain model to be built (management or design).

Step 2 - Supplier Data

We proceed to specify location of suppliers, products offered, supply cost of products, and maximum amounts (capacity).

Products can be selected from an available database or can be added to the database.


In this case, we also have three suppliers that offer waste at different costs and amounts. You can drag those markers and double click to modify inputs.

Step 3 - Technology Data

Here we specify locations of technology providers and candidates, as well as technology specifications (type, costs, and maximum processing capacity).

Each technology follows a pathway to transform input products into other products.

Technologies can be selected from an available database or can be added to the database.


In this example, we have two technology providers and one technology candidate at different locations. You can drag those markers and double click to modify inputs.

Step 4 - Consumer Data

We specify location of consumers, type of product requested, purchase cost of products, and maximum amounts.


In this example, we have four consumers at different locations. You can drag those markers and double click to modify inputs.


Step 5 – Transport Data

Here we specify data for transportation providers.

All possible transport routes are automatically generated from supplier, consumer, and technology data provided.


Here the left map shows the transportation providers for waste, and the right map is for derived products. Click on the routes to get details.
